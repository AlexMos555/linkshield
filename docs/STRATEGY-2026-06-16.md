## Details + ship plans

### #1 — Credential-form action-host mismatch + password-field heuristic

**Category:** `detection-quality` · **Impact:** 9/10 · **Differentiation:** 7/10 · **Effort:** 4w

**One-line:** Detect credential phishing at the moment a password field appears, by comparing form action-host vs visible host and known brand identity.

**Why this rank:** Highest impact per week of work in the whole inventory. Credential phishing is the #1 monetised attack and our ML model is explicitly URL-only (no page content). This single signal closes the biggest detection gap and unlocks four downstream items (honeypot shield, credential guardian, why-this-is-dangerous explainer, BitB detection). Cheap, deterministic, no model retraining needed.

**Ship plan:** W1: design heuristic spec (password field + form action host + visible brand). W2: implement extension-side detector, wire to backend verdict. W3: shadow-mode on top-1k phish corpus, tune thresholds. W4: ship to extension beta, surface in block page reasons.

**Competitor parity:** matches Bitdefender TrafficLight credential warnings, beats Norton/McAfee (URL-only), opens credential-guardian + honeypot-shield

Pairs with item #1: the form-mismatch detector + visual brand match together is a near-perfect credential-phish classifier. Favicon mmh3 alone is the 'Shodan trick' and lands in W2.

**Ship plan:** W1: stand up headless-render worker (Playwright on Railway), favicon mmh3 lib. W2: ship favicon-only matcher as standalone signal (covers ~40% of brand clones). W3: build pHash gallery for top-200 brands. W4: integrate pHash signal into scorer with confidence band.

## 5 strategic horizon themes

- From URL-only to credential-aware: items 1, 2, 7, 8, 11 collectively transform Cleanway from a URL-reputation scanner into an active credential-leak shield. This is the single biggest product reframe in the plan and the most defensible against well-resourced incumbents because it pulls protection forward to the input event, not the navigation event.
- Receipts, not red pages: items 4, 10, 12, 16 share a single thesis — Cleanway wins trust by showing its work. Annotated screenshots, public scorecards, conformal confidence bands, and a quarterly transparency report with a published false-positive rate compound into a 'verifiable security' brand that Norton/Bitdefender structurally cannot copy without exposing their own numbers.
- Persona-first product, not features-first: items 3, 9, 15, 19 commit to skill-level segmentation as a structural choice. Grandma Mode + Kids/Regular/Pro + culturally-localized explanations + Family fan-out treat the user's competence and context as a first-class product axis. Every competitor ships one UI; we ship four, in ten cultures.
- Reachability and time-to-protect: items 5, 6, 18 close the boring-but-fatal gaps — fresh feeds (so we don't miss the phish), one-tap install (so we don't lose the user at onboarding), and full browser-store coverage (so the user has us where they are). These are the items that turn a great detection engine into a usable one.
- Predictive and pre-emptive defense: items 17 and the conformal-band thread (12, 16) signal a strategic shift from reactive blocking ('this domain was reported') to predictive blocking ('this domain doesn't exist yet but it will target you'). This is the long-horizon theme that — combined with the Family fan-out — defines Cleanway's 5-year category.
