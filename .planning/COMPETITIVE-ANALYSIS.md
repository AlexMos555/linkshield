# LinkShield — Competitive Analysis

## Executive summary

The anti-phishing market in 2026 is fragmented across four categories: browser extensions (Guardio, Netcraft, Malwarebytes), VPN-bundled protection (NordVPN, Bitdefender), mobile security apps (Norton, Avira, Bitdefender), and B2B security awareness platforms (KnowBe4, Proofpoint). No single product delivers automatic, cross-platform link protection with a plain-language privacy audit. This is LinkShield's wedge.

## Market gaps LinkShield exploits

**Gap 1: No one does plain-language privacy audit.**
Netcraft shows technical data. Privacy Badger blocks trackers. Nobody translates "14 trackers, canvas fingerprinting, 34 third-party cookies" into "This site is tracking you across the internet and can identify your device."

**Gap 2: No cross-platform "set and forget" protection.**
Guardio is closest but doesn't cover all mobile apps. NordVPN covers OS-level but requires their VPN. Nobody offers extension + mobile VPN + email proxy under one subscription.

**Gap 3: No anti-phishing product shows its value proactively.**
All products are silent when working. Nobody sends weekly reports showing "here's what we blocked." Nobody scans your inbox to show past threats you missed. This is why churn is high in security products.

**Gap 4: "Protect my family" is underserved.**
Guardio has a family plan (shared subscription) but no real-time alerting between family members. Nobody lets you see when a threat was blocked on mom's phone. This is both a use case and a growth mechanism.

**Gap 5: SMB phishing simulation is overpriced.**
KnowBe4 starts at 25 users, $16+/user/yr, enterprise sales cycle. Small businesses (5-50 employees) are priced out or over-served. A self-service $3.99/user/mo product with combined protection + simulation fills this gap.

**Gap 6: VPN conflict is unaddressed.**
Many anti-phishing mobile solutions rely on VPN. If user already has a VPN, they can't use the product. Nobody auto-detects and falls back to DNS. This blocks ~30% of the mobile addressable market.
